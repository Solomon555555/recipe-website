Fufu and Light Soup

Fufu and light soup is one of the most traditional and respected meals in Ghana. Fufu is made by pounding boiled cassava and plantain until it becomes smooth and stretchy. Light soup is a flavorful broth prepared with tomatoes, peppers, onions, and spices. This dish is enjoyed across all regions of Ghana.

The soup is prepared by boiling meat or fish with blended tomatoes, onions, garlic, and pepper. Seasonings are added to enhance the flavor. The soup is allowed to simmer until it becomes rich and aromatic. Fufu is usually eaten by pinching small portions and dipping them into the soup.

Fufu and light soup is commonly served during family gatherings and special occasions. It is filling, nutritious, and deeply rooted in Ghanaian culture.
